About The Simpsons Hit & Run

What is
The Simpsons Hit & Run?

The Simpsons Hit & Run is a 2003 action-adventure driving game developed by Radical Entertainment and published by Vivendi Universal Games.

Based on the popular American animated sitcom The Simpsons and is the twenty-second installment in the Simpsons series of video games. The game follows the Simpson family and their friend Apu as they uncover an alien conspiracy in Springfield caused by Kang and Kodos.

The gameplay focuses on exploration, missions, and racing, with players often interacting with supporting characters on timed quests. It also features elements found in role-playing games, such as explorable worlds and side tasks.

Who are the Developers?

The developer Radical Entertainment received the rights to create games for The Simpsons franchise after demonstrating a playable prototype. They had previously released The Simpsons: Road Rage in 2001 but decided to steer the franchise's video game series in a different direction with Hit & Run.

The development team consisted of around 60 people and was led by producer Vlad Ceraldi, designer Joe McGinn, and artist Yayoi Maruno-Chorney. They collaborated with the show's writers and cast to craft the story and dialogue, taking inspiration from the Grand Theft Auto series for the open-world design and nuanced character development.

Radical Entertainment has also developed classic titles such as Destiny and the Prototype Series.
